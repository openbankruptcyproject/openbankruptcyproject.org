Section 02About Bankruptcy Trustees

The federal bankruptcy system uses several categories of trustee. The United States Trustee is a Department of Justice official supervising administration of cases in 21 regions covering 88 districts. Chapter 7 panel trustees are appointed case-by-case to liquidate assets. Standing Chapter 13 trustees serve all Chapter 13 cases in a division on a percentage-fee basis. Subchapter V trustees are appointed by the US Trustee from a regional panel to facilitate small-business reorganizations under SBRA. Alabama and North Carolina districts use the legacy Bankruptcy Administrator program rather than the USTP.

OBP’s per-trustee pages are neutral public-record entries — they identify each trustee’s role and the statutory framework that governs it, and link to the USTP regional office or Bankruptcy Administrator office that supervises trustee activity. OBP does not publish per-trustee case counts, lists of cases administered, or qualitative evaluations of trustee conduct.
