Public-record federal case captions. No account needed.

PACER (Public Access to Court Electronic Records) is operated by the Administrative Office of the U.S. Courts. Statutory authority under 28 USC § 1913 and 28 USC § 1930 lets the Judiciary set access fees. The fee is currently $0.10 per page with a $3 cap per document, billed quarterly. Account holders who incur less than a fixed quarterly threshold are not charged. Court opinions are free.

The PACER fee problem

PACER (Public Access to Court Electronic Records) is the official federal courts system for accessing court documents. It charges $0.10 per page, with most documents capped at $3.00. Searches are also $0.10. While there's a quarterly waiver for users billing under $30, larger users pay substantial fees — the courts collect over $145 million per year from PACER.

Congress originally authorized PACER under the E-Government Act of 2002 to be self-sustaining only — not a profit center. The growing surplus has been the subject of long-running litigation: National Veterans Legal Services Program v. United States, 968 F.3d 1340 (Fed. Cir. 2020), held that the courts had been charging more than the statute allowed.

What this site does instead

We publish the public-record case caption metadata (debtor name, case number, chapter, filing date, district) for free. This information is constitutionally-protected speech under Florida Star v. B.J.F., 491 U.S. 524 (1989), and Cox Broadcasting Corp. v. Cohn, 420 U.S. 469 (1975), and we publish it without fees, signup, or ads.

For full document access:

  • RECAP / CourtListener — operated by the Free Law Project, a nonprofit. Contributed PACER documents are available for free. Install the RECAP browser extension; any PACER docs you pay for get archived for everyone.
  • Court self-service centers — most federal bankruptcy courts have public-access terminals in their clerk's offices for free document viewing.
  • PACER fee waiver — under $30/quarter is waived automatically.

What we cover

  • Case captions from PACER + RECAP across 95+ federal bankruptcy districts
  • Case number, chapter, filing date for each indexed case
  • Link to free document access on CourtListener where RECAP coverage exists
  • Link to PACER (paywall) where documents are PACER-only
  • Cross-search with SBA loan data (PPP + EIDL) for businesses that received pandemic-era loans

The case-caption data we publish is not the same as the full PACER docket. We don't republish PACER documents (RECAP does that). We index the caption-level metadata to make name-based discovery free.

When you still need PACER

PACER remains necessary for:

  • The complete docket entry list for a case (we show case caption, not entry-by-entry filings)
  • Document downloads when the doc isn't in the RECAP Archive
  • Filing your own documents (attorneys and pro se filers must file through CM/ECF/PACER)
  • Sealed or restricted documents (we never publish these; we wouldn't have access either)

For everything else — basic "did X file bankruptcy?" lookups, conflict-checks, background research, journalism — the free index here handles the use case.
