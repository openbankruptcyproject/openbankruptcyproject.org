Confirming a Plan of Reorganization

Plan Confirmation

Plan confirmation is the gateway through which a reorganizing debtor exits bankruptcy with a discharge and a binding compromise of pre-petition obligations. Each of the four reorganization chapters has its own confirmation standard, voting rules, and creditor-protection tests. These four deep-dives unpack the framework for chapter 11, chapter 12, chapter 13, and Subchapter V of chapter 11.

About the confirmation process

Plan confirmation in bankruptcy serves two functions simultaneously: it binds creditors to a compromise of their pre-petition rights, and it triggers the discharge that gives the debtor a fresh start. The Bankruptcy Code prescribes detailed substantive and procedural standards to ensure that the compromise is fair and that creditors had meaningful opportunity to vote and object.

The confirmation framework is built on a shared scaffolding of feasibility, good faith, best-interests-of-creditors, and (in chapter 11 and Subchapter V) absolute-priority or fair-and-equitable rules. But each chapter has its own statutory section enumerating the confirmation requirements: section 1129 for chapter 11, section 1225 for chapter 12, section 1325 for chapter 13, and the cross-referenced sections 1191 and 1129(a) for Subchapter V. The differences across chapters are not cosmetic - they reflect Congress's judgment that different debtor types warrant different protections for different creditor classes.

The mechanics of confirmation also differ. Chapter 11 typically requires a disclosure statement under section 1125, solicitation of votes from impaired classes under section 1126, and a confirmation hearing that may be contested by any party in interest. Chapter 12 and chapter 13 do not require a disclosure statement (the plan itself plus the section 341 meeting serves the disclosure function), and confirmation is heard before the chapter 12/13 trustee and the bankruptcy judge on a streamlined timeline. Subchapter V eliminates the disclosure statement and the creditors' committee while preserving most other chapter 11 confirmation standards in modified form.

Chapter-by-chapter confirmation deep-dives

  • Chapter 11 Plan ConfirmationThe sixteen section 1129(a) confirmation requirements, the section 1129(b) cramdown alternative, absolute-priority and new-value, and the impaired-accepting-class requirement.
  • Chapter 12 Plan ConfirmationThe section 1225(a) confirmation requirements for family-farmer and family-fisherman reorganizations, the section 1222(b)(9) cramdown of secured creditors over collateral value, and the standing-trustee role.
  • Chapter 13 Plan ConfirmationThe section 1325(a) confirmation requirements, the section 1325(b) disposable-income test, the applicable-commitment-period rules, and the section 1322(b)(2) anti-modification restriction for principal-residence mortgages.
  • Subchapter V Plan ConfirmationSections 1181-1195 and the cross-referenced sections 1129(a) requirements, the section 1191(b) cramdown without an impaired-accepting class, the section 1191(c) fair-and-equitable test, and the SBRA-modified absolute-priority rule.
Voting note: Under section 1126(c), a class of claims accepts the plan if it is accepted by holders of at least two-thirds in amount and more than one-half in number of the allowed claims actually voting. Non-voters do not count for or against acceptance. Vote solicitation must comply with the section 1125 disclosure-statement requirements in chapter 11.
