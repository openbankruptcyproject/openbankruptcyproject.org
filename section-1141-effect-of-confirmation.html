What Is Section 1141?

Section 1141 of Title 11 of the United States Code (Effect of Confirmation) is a foundational provision of the Bankruptcy Code. It makes a confirmed Chapter 11 plan binding on the debtor, all creditors, and equity holders, whether or not they accepted the plan. Confirmation effectively replaces pre-petition rights with the plan's terms.

Official citation: 11 U.S.C. § 1141

Key Provisions of Section 1141

Key effects of confirmation:

  • 1141(a): The plan binds the debtor, all creditors, equity holders, and any entity acquiring property under the plan.
  • 1141(b): Property dealt with by the plan is free and clear of all claims and interests of creditors and equity holders, except as provided in the plan.
  • 1141(c): Property of the estate vests in the debtor (unless the plan provides otherwise).
  • 1141(d) - Discharge: Confirmation discharges the debtor from pre-confirmation debts, except those specified in the plan or excepted from discharge by law. However, individual debtors in Chapter 11 do not receive a discharge until all plan payments are completed.

How This Affects You

Post-confirmation realities:

  • The plan replaces all pre-petition obligations. Creditors' rights are now defined by the plan, not their original agreements.
  • Property vests in the reorganized debtor, giving it a fresh start to operate going forward.
  • For individual Chapter 11 debtors, the discharge is delayed until plan completion (unlike corporate debtors who get an immediate discharge at confirmation).

Related Bankruptcy Code Sections

Section 1141 works in conjunction with several other provisions of the Bankruptcy Code:

  • Section 1129 - Confirmation requirements
  • Section 1193 - Post-confirmation modification

Understanding how these sections interact is critical for anyone navigating the bankruptcy process, whether as a debtor, creditor, or attorney.
