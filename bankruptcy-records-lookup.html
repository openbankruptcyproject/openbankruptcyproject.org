Public-record federal case captions from PACER + RECAP.

U.S. bankruptcy filings are court records that originate on PACER. Free or low-cost mirrors include the RECAP Archive on CourtListener, the Free Law Project's docket pages, and aggregated case-index sites such as the Open Bankruptcy Project. These mirrors typically cover dockets and many filed PDFs but do not always have every sealed or restricted document available on PACER.

What records are searchable

Each result includes the case caption (debtor name), case number, chapter (7, 11, 13, or Subchapter V), filing date, and the district court. Where the document trail has been captured by the free RECAP Archive, we link to free document access on CourtListener. Where only PACER has the documents, we link to PACER with a clear paywall notice.

The index covers all 95+ federal bankruptcy districts. Consumer cases (Chapters 7 and 13), business cases (Chapter 11), Subchapter V small-business cases, and Chapter 12 family farmer cases all appear.

We focus on the search-and-discovery layer because that's where free public-record access matters most. For detailed docket entries and document downloads, use PACER or contribute to the RECAP Archive (free, via browser extension).

What you can find

Common use cases:

  • Background checks — verifying whether a contractor, business partner, or landlord has filed bankruptcy
  • Creditor research — checking if a debt counterparty has filed for bankruptcy protection
  • Personal lookup — confirming your own bankruptcy case appears as expected
  • Genealogy / family history — finding bankruptcy filings of ancestors or relatives
  • Journalism / research — investigating company financial troubles or bankruptcy patterns
  • Legal practice — quick conflicts-checks before taking on a client or party
