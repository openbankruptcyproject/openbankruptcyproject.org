Section 02About Bankruptcy Judges

Article I bankruptcy judges are appointed for renewable 14-year terms by the United States Court of Appeals for the circuit in which the district sits (28 U.S.C. § 152), not by the President or with Senate confirmation. This makes bankruptcy-court appointments structurally distinct from Article III district-court and circuit-court appointments. Each bankruptcy court publishes its sitting roster at its uscourts.gov sub-domain, and the FJC Biographical Directory of Federal Judges is the authoritative national index.

OBP’s per-judge pages are neutral public-record entries — they identify each judge’s role and link to the court’s own biographical page, the court’s Local Rules, and the wider district context. OBP does not publish per-judge case counts (the FJC Integrated Database does not publish those numbers), lists of cases handled, or qualitative evaluations of rulings.
