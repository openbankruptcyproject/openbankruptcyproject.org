Section 02About the Court

United States Bankruptcy Court for the Northern District of Texas is one of 94 federal bankruptcy courts in the United States. Article I bankruptcy judges are appointed for renewable 14-year terms by the United States Court of Appeals for the circuit in which the district sits (see 28 U.S.C. § 152). The court’s primary public-facing presence is the official uscourts.gov sub-domain at https://www.txnb.uscourts.gov, which publishes the roster of sitting judges, hearing calendars, chambers procedures, the local rules of practice, and electronic-filing instructions.

OBP does not duplicate biographical narrative from the FJC public-record directory. The court’s own bio page for Scott W. Everett is linked below.

Section 07Privacy Rule (FRBP 9037) Context

Federal Rule of Bankruptcy Procedure 9037 requires that Social Security Numbers, dates of birth, financial account numbers, and minor-child identifiers be redacted in bankruptcy filings. The Federal Judicial Center’s April 2024 empirical study, Unredacted Social Security Numbers in Federal Court PACER Documents, documented systemic compliance shortfalls across the federal court system — particularly in proof-of-claim filings, which the study found to be 98% noncompliant. The FJC study does not publish per-district or per-judge breakdowns. OBP cites the FJC study only as national context; OBP does not publish per-judge or per-district Rule 9037 compliance numbers.

Primary source: Unredacted Social Security Numbers in Federal Court PACER Documents (FJC, April 2024).
