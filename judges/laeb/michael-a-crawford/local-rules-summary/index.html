Section 01Overview

This page orients readers to the local-rules ecosystem governing the chamber of Michael A. Crawford, Judge of the United States Bankruptcy Court for the Eastern District of Louisiana (E.D. La.). It is a navigational guide — not a chambers-specific rules digest — and identifies the layered rule-sources every matter before this judge passes through: the Federal Rules of Bankruptcy Procedure, this district’s Local Rules of Practice, the court’s general orders, any chambers standing orders published on the court’s website, and the underlying United States Bankruptcy Code at Title 11.

OBP does not summarize rulings, characterize a judge’s approach, or publish a list of cases handled. The framework below is the same framework that governs every chamber in the E.D. La. and, with district-specific variations, every U.S. bankruptcy court nationally.

Section 02The Rule Stack in This Chamber

Practitioners appearing before Michael A. Crawford work within four layered sources of procedural authority. Each layer supplements (and may narrow) the layer above it.

1. Title 11 of the United States Code — The Bankruptcy Code

The substantive statute. Title 11 governs case eligibility, property of the estate, the automatic stay, claims allowance, discharge, trustee duties, and chapter-specific machinery. Title 28 supplies the jurisdictional and administrative provisions (including 28 U.S.C. § 152, which establishes Article I bankruptcy judgeships).

2. Federal Rules of Bankruptcy Procedure (FRBP)

National procedural rules. The FRBP govern petition practice (Rule 1007), service (Rule 7004), contested matters (Rule 9014), adversary proceedings (Part VII), plan confirmation, fee applications (Rule 2016), withdrawal of counsel (Rule 9010 plus the district’s local rule), and the FRBP 9037 redaction requirements applicable to every filing.

3. E.D. La. Local Rules of Practice

The district-specific overlay the United States Bankruptcy Court for the Eastern District of Louisiana publishes and amends. Local Rules govern what the FRBP leaves to local practice — page limits, required certifications, hearing-request mechanics, electronic filing, local forms, and any attorney-admission rules specific to this district. Every matter before Michael A. Crawford is governed by these Local Rules unless a chambers procedure narrows them further.

4. Chambers Standing Orders and Procedures

Individual judges may publish chambers-specific standing orders (calendar protocols, exhibit submission, agreed-order formatting, chapter-13 plan templates, scheduling defaults). Where published, these appear on the judge’s page within the court’s own website. Where not published, default practice is the E.D. La. Local Rules plus the FRBP.

Section 03E.D. La. Local Rules — Deeper Reference

Section 04Frequently-Encountered Local-Rule Topics

Across U.S. bankruptcy courts, certain Local Rule topics recur in nearly every district. Numbering differs by court; the procedural areas are uniform. In the E.D. La., expect Local Rules addressing:

  • Attorney admission and CM/ECF registration.
  • Entry of appearance and withdrawal of counsel (FRBP 9010 plus the district’s Local Rule).
  • Motion practice and response timing (hearing requests, page limits, proposed orders).
  • Fee disclosures and applications (FRBP 2016(a)/(b) plus any Local Rule on form or schedule).
  • Chapter 13 plan and confirmation practice (model plan, confirmation timing).
  • Adversary proceeding mechanics (pretrial defaults, local discovery protocols).
  • FRBP 9037 privacy redaction (national standard plus any local supplementary guidance).

Topic-area guide, not a Local Rule digest. Verify current numbering and amendment dates against the E.D. La. website.
