Section 01Why District-Wide Aggregates

Pamela W. McAfee is one of the Article I bankruptcy judges sitting in the United States Bankruptcy Court for the Eastern District of North Carolina. The Federal Judicial Center Integrated Database (FJC IDB), the authoritative public-record aggregate source for federal bankruptcy filings, does not publish case counts or outcome statistics disaggregated by individual judge chamber. The closest public-record context for any one chamber is the district-wide aggregate in which that chamber sits.

This page therefore reports E.D.N.C. totals as a whole. The figures below describe the court, not Pamela W. McAfee as an individual judge, and include cases from every chamber in the district. OBP does not characterize any individual chamber.

Method note. The FJC IDB is keyed at the case level, not the chamber level. Per-judge case-disposition extracts are not part of the FJC’s public release, so OBP does not approximate per-judge statistics from non-IDB sources.

Section 04E.D.N.C. Prior-Filer Rate (Chapter 13)

Of Chapter 13 cases in E.D.N.C. with a coded prior-filing flag in the FJC IDB, 17.48% (57,672 cases) carry a recorded prior bankruptcy filing. This metric is relevant to 11 U.S.C. § 1328(f) discharge eligibility.

This is a district-wide rate. The FJC IDB does not flag prior-filing status broken out by individual judge chamber, so OBP does not attribute the prior-filer rate to Pamela W. McAfee or any other chamber.

Section 09Privacy Rule (FRBP 9037) Context

FRBP 9037 requires that SSNs, dates of birth, financial account numbers, and minor-child identifiers be redacted in bankruptcy filings. The Federal Judicial Center’s April 2024 study found 98% noncompliance in proof-of-claim filings nationally. The FJC study does not publish per-district or per-judge breakdowns. OBP cites the study only as national context.

Primary source: Unredacted Social Security Numbers in Federal Court PACER Documents (FJC, April 2024).
