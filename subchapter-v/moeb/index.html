Section 01What Subchapter V Is

Subchapter V of Chapter 11 of the United States Bankruptcy Code is a streamlined reorganization track for small-business debtors. The framework eliminates several procedural requirements that traditionally raise the cost of Chapter 11, including the appointment of a creditors' committee in most cases and the absolute priority rule when the plan is crammed down on a non-consenting class.

Who files Subchapter V: small-business debtors meeting the aggregate non-contingent liquidated debt threshold in 11 U.S.C. § 1182(1), elected at the time of filing on the voluntary petition. Subchapter V was added by the Small Business Reorganization Act of 2019 and took effect February 19 2020.

Typical timeline: The debtor must file a plan within 90 days of the order for relief under 11 U.S.C. § 1189(b). A standing Subchapter V trustee is appointed under 11 U.S.C. § 1183 to facilitate plan development and confirmation. Cramdown is available under 11 U.S.C. § 1191(b) without the absolute priority rule.

For statutory detail and procedural anchors, see Subchapter V statute reference.

Section 02Subchapter V Volume in E.D. Mo.

United States Bankruptcy Court for the Eastern District of Missouri has 2,363 Chapter 11 cases on file in the FJC IDB, or 0.5% of the district's 435,440 total federal bankruptcy filings. Subchapter V is a procedural sub-election within Chapter 11 and was created by the Small Business Reorganization Act of 2019, effective February 19 2020. The IDB began tracking Subchapter V elections as a separate field with that effective date.

Districts vary substantially in Chapter 11 activity. Concentrated venues for corporate Chapter 11 (such as D. Del. and S.D.N.Y.) attract larger debtors and a higher absolute Subchapter V case count even though small-business reorganization is geographically distributed.

Section 05District Characteristics

Federal bankruptcy filings in E.D. Mo. are administered by the United States Bankruptcy Court for the Eastern District of Missouri, sitting in the Eighth Circuit of the United States Courts of Appeals.

Subchapter V cases in this district are administered by a trustee appointed under 11 U.S.C. § 1183 from the standing Subchapter V trustee panel maintained by the United States Trustee Program.

Subchapter V eligibility under 11 U.S.C. § 1182(1) is determined as of the petition date and depends on aggregate non-contingent liquidated debt. The debt threshold has been adjusted multiple times by Congress since the Small Business Reorganization Act of 2019 took effect.

Sub V trustees in this district may charge for services consistent with 11 U.S.C. § 330(a) and Bankruptcy Rule 2016, with fee applications subject to notice to the United States Trustee and to creditors with standing under § 330(a)(2).
