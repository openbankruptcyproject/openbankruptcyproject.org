Federal Rule of Bankruptcy Procedure 4002

Rule 4002: Duties of the Debtor

The Rule 4002(a) general duties and the Rule 4002(b) specific document and identification requirements at the Section 341 meeting.

Verbatim text of Rule 4002(a)

In addition to performing other duties prescribed by the Code and rules, the debtor shall (1) attend and submit to an examination at the times ordered by the court; (2) attend the hearing on a complaint objecting to discharge and testify, if called as a witness; (3) inform the trustee immediately in writing as to the location of real property in which the debtor has an interest and the name and address of every person holding money or property subject to the debtor's withdrawal or order if a schedule of property has not yet been filed pursuant to Rule 1007; (4) cooperate with the trustee in the preparation of an inventory, the examination of proofs of claim, and the administration of the estate; and (5) file a statement of any change of the debtor's address. Fed. R. Bankr. P. 4002(a).

The structure of Rule 4002

Federal Rule of Bankruptcy Procedure 4002 is the procedural codification of the debtor's affirmative duties in a bankruptcy case. The rule has two principal subdivisions:

  • Rule 4002(a) sets out five general categories of duty: attendance at examinations, attendance and testimony at discharge hearings, immediate disclosure of real property and asset-holders, cooperation with the trustee, and notice of address changes.
  • Rule 4002(b) specifies the documents and identification the debtor must provide at the Section 341 meeting of creditors, including the personal-photo-ID requirement.

Rule 4002 supplements - but does not replace - the substantive debtor duties prescribed by 11 U.S.C. Section 521(a), which establishes the underlying statutory obligations.

The five general duties under Rule 4002(a)

(1) Attendance at examinations

The debtor must attend and submit to examination at any time ordered by the court. This includes the Section 341 meeting of creditors, any Rule 2004 examination (the bankruptcy equivalent of a free-ranging deposition), and any other examination ordered in the case. Failure to attend is grounds for dismissal of the case under 11 U.S.C. Section 707(a) (Chapter 7) or denial of discharge under Section 727(a)(6) (refusal to obey a lawful order of the court).

(2) Attendance and testimony at discharge hearings

If a complaint objecting to discharge is filed (under Section 727 in Chapter 7 or Section 1328 in Chapter 13), the debtor must attend the hearing and testify if called as a witness. The debtor's failure to attend may result in the objection being sustained.

(3) Immediate disclosure of real property and asset-holders

If the debtor has not yet filed schedules under Rule 1007, the debtor must, immediately upon the appointment of the trustee, inform the trustee in writing of:

  • The location of all real property in which the debtor has an interest
  • The name and address of every person holding money or property subject to the debtor's withdrawal or order

This is a pre-schedule disclosure obligation - it applies in the gap between commencement of the case and the filing of the formal schedules. Its purpose is to ensure that the trustee can secure assets before they can be transferred or dissipated.

(4) Cooperation with the trustee

The debtor must cooperate with the trustee in the preparation of an inventory, the examination of proofs of claim, and the administration of the estate. This is a broad and ongoing duty. Specific manifestations include:

  • Producing the debtor's books, records, and documents on request
  • Appearing for examination on request
  • Providing access to real property and physical premises for inspection or marketing
  • Answering inquiries from the trustee and the United States trustee regarding the debtor's financial affairs
  • Executing documents necessary to perfect the trustee's title to estate property

(5) Notice of address changes

The debtor must file a statement of any change in the debtor's address. This is essential to ensure that notices required by the rules reach the debtor and to permit the trustee to maintain communication.

Rule 4002(b) - documents and identification at the Section 341 meeting

Rule 4002(b) addresses what the debtor must bring to and produce at the Section 341 meeting of creditors. The subdivision was added in part to standardize practice across districts and in part to implement the more rigorous documentation requirements that emerged from the 2005 amendments to the Bankruptcy Code (BAPCPA).

(1) Personal identification

The debtor must bring to the Section 341 meeting:

  • A photo identification issued by a governmental unit, sufficient to permit the trustee to verify that the debtor is the person who signed the petition. The standard form is a state driver's license or a state-issued identification card. A U.S. passport is also accepted. Some districts accept additional forms by local rule.
  • Evidence of the debtor's social security number, or a written statement that no such evidence exists. The standard form is the original social security card; a W-2 form or a tax return showing the number is typically accepted as a substitute when the card is unavailable.

The two-document identification protocol is universal. A debtor who appears without acceptable identification will typically be continued to a later meeting date, with the trustee or the United States trustee noting on the record the failure to appear with proper identification.

(2) Financial information

Rule 4002(b)(2) requires the debtor to bring to the Section 341 meeting and produce on request:

  • Evidence of current income (typically pay stubs or an equivalent), unless previously produced
  • Statements for each depository and investment account in which the debtor has an interest, for the period including the petition date
  • Documentation of monthly expenses claimed on Schedule J if the trustee requests verification

(3) Tax returns

Rule 4002(b)(3) implements the BAPCPA tax-return production requirements. The debtor must produce, at the request of the trustee or the United States trustee:

  • The most recently filed federal income tax return (or, if no return was filed, a written statement to that effect)
  • Federal income tax returns for the period before the petition, as required by other rules and orders

Tax returns must also be made available to a requesting creditor under 11 U.S.C. Section 521(e)(2), subject to confidentiality safeguards.

The Section 521(a) statutory backbone

Rule 4002 implements the broader statutory duties of 11 U.S.C. Section 521(a). The rule's document-production and cooperation requirements track the statutory text. Section 521(a) requires the debtor to:

  • File a list of creditors, schedules of assets and liabilities, a schedule of current income and expenditures, a schedule of executory contracts and unexpired leases, and a statement of financial affairs
  • If an individual debtor's debts are primarily consumer debts, file a statement of intention with respect to property securing those debts
  • Cooperate with the trustee as necessary to enable the trustee to perform the trustee's duties
  • Surrender to the trustee all property of the estate and any recorded information relating to property of the estate
  • Appear at the meeting required by Section 343

The Rule 4002 cooperation duty (4002(a)(4)) is the procedural counterpart of the Section 521(a)(3) statutory cooperation duty. The two operate together.

Consequences of noncompliance

Failure to comply with Rule 4002 or Section 521 has direct and substantial consequences. Available responses include:

Dismissal of the case

Under 11 U.S.C. Section 707(a) (Chapter 7) or Section 1307(c) (Chapter 13), dismissal "for cause" includes failure of the debtor to perform the duties required by the Code and rules. The standard for dismissal varies by circuit; some require notice and hearing, and a graduated response (e.g., a deadline to cure before dismissal).

Denial of discharge

Section 727(a)(6) authorizes denial of discharge in Chapter 7 for refusal to obey a lawful order of the court. Section 1328(a) conditions discharge in Chapter 13 on completion of plan payments and compliance with the rules.

Conversion or other relief

In Chapter 13, repeated failure to cooperate or to produce required documents is grounds for the trustee's motion to dismiss or convert under Section 1307(c).

Adverse evidentiary inferences

A debtor who refuses to produce documents or to answer questions at the Section 341 meeting may be subject to adverse evidentiary inferences in any related contested matter (e.g., claim objection, motion for relief from stay, complaint to determine dischargeability).

Common procedural posture

Section 341 meeting attendance

The debtor appears at the Section 341 meeting with photo ID, social security card, the most recently filed tax return, and pay stubs for the 60 days before the petition. The trustee verifies identification on the record, reviews the schedules with the debtor under oath, and may ask questions about specific transactions, asset values, and income sources. The meeting typically lasts five to fifteen minutes in a routine Chapter 7 case.

Trustee document request

Following the Section 341 meeting, the Chapter 7 trustee identifies a potential asset (e.g., a personal-injury claim, an account receivable, a non-exempt vehicle) and requests additional documents under Rule 4002(a)(4) cooperation. The debtor produces the documents through counsel within the deadline set by the trustee. Failure to produce may prompt a motion under Section 707(a) for dismissal for cause.

Practical impact

Rule 4002 is the procedural floor of the debtor's relationship with the trustee and the court. Every Chapter 7 and Chapter 13 case implicates the rule from the date of filing through the date of discharge. Debtor's counsel typically prepares the debtor for the Section 341 meeting by reviewing the schedules in detail, confirming that the photo ID and social security documentation are in order, and rehearsing the trustee's standard line of questions. Trustees, in turn, build their case-administration practice around the cooperation and document-production requirements - much of what a Chapter 7 trustee does in the first 30 days of a case is enforcement of Rule 4002 and Section 521(a).

Bring the photo ID and the social security card. The most common Section 341 problem is a debtor who appears without acceptable identification. The trustee will continue the meeting; the case clock effectively pauses; and creditors receive renewed notice. The simplest way to avoid the problem is for counsel to confirm, in writing and by reminder the day before, that the debtor will bring both documents.
