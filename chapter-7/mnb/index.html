Section 01What Chapter 7 Is

Chapter 7 of the United States Bankruptcy Code provides for the liquidation of a debtor's non-exempt property by a court-appointed trustee, with the net proceeds distributed to creditors. In exchange, individual debtors typically receive a discharge of most pre-petition unsecured debts under 11 U.S.C. § 727, subject to the exceptions in 11 U.S.C. § 523.

Who files Chapter 7: individuals and small businesses with limited disposable income, or with primarily non-exempt assets the debtor is willing to surrender to the bankruptcy estate in exchange for a discharge.

Typical timeline: Most consumer Chapter 7 cases close within 90 to 120 days of the petition date. A 11 U.S.C. § 341 meeting of creditors is typically set 21 to 40 days after the petition, and a discharge order under 11 U.S.C. § 727 is normally entered approximately 60 days after the first scheduled § 341 meeting if no objections are filed.

Section 05District Characteristics
