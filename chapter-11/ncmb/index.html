Section 01What Chapter 11 Is

Chapter 11 of the United States Bankruptcy Code authorizes reorganization, typically of business debtors. A debtor-in-possession retains control of operations and proposes a plan of reorganization that, if confirmed under 11 U.S.C. § 1129, binds creditors to its restructured payment terms.

Who files Chapter 11: businesses (and a smaller number of high-debt individuals) seeking to reorganize operations and restructure debt while continuing as going concerns. Small-business cases may elect Subchapter V under 11 U.S.C. § 1181 et seq. if eligibility thresholds are met.

Typical timeline: Chapter 11 timelines vary widely. Subchapter V cases must file a plan within 90 days under 11 U.S.C. § 1189(b). Traditional Chapter 11 cases may run for months or years through plan negotiation, disclosure-statement approval, confirmation, and post-confirmation administration.

For statutory detail and procedural anchors, see Chapter 11 statute reference.

Section 05District Characteristics

Federal bankruptcy filings in M.D.N.C. are administered by the United States Bankruptcy Court for the Middle District of North Carolina, sitting in the Fourth Circuit of the United States Courts of Appeals.

Chapter 11 debtors-in-possession remain in control of operations subject to oversight by the United States Trustee under 28 U.S.C. § 586(a)(3) and Bankruptcy Rule 2015.
