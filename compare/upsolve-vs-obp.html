Which Free Bankruptcy Tool Is Better? A Fair, Side-by-Side Comparison

Overview

Both Upsolve and the Open Bankruptcy Project (OBP) are nonprofit organizations providing free bankruptcy resources. But they serve very different purposes. Upsolve helps low-income individuals prepare Chapter 7 bankruptcy filings through a guided app. OBP provides empirical research, screening tools, and educational content covering all bankruptcy chapters across a network of 161 specialized domains.

Neither is "better" in an absolute sense - it depends on what you need.

When to Use Upsolve

  • You have decided to file Chapter 7 bankruptcy and need help preparing the forms
  • Your income is below the median for your state (means test eligible)
  • You want a step-by-step guided process to generate court-ready documents
  • You are comfortable entering personal financial information into an online tool

Upsolve has helped thousands of people file Chapter 7 without an attorney. Their app walks you through the entire process, from the means test to generating the petition and schedules.

When to Use Open Bankruptcy Project

  • You are researching whether bankruptcy is right for you before making any decisions
  • You need information about Chapter 13, Chapter 11, or Subchapter V - not just Chapter 7
  • You want to understand discharge eligibility timelines (the 1328(f) screener)
  • You are a researcher, attorney, or journalist who needs empirical bankruptcy data
  • You do not want to share personal financial information with any platform
  • You want state-specific or city-specific bankruptcy guides

OBP is the largest independent bankruptcy research network, analyzing 4.9 million federal cases. Its tools help you understand the system before you engage with it - without ever asking who you are.

Can You Use Both?

Absolutely. Many people start with OBP to research their options, understand discharge eligibility, and learn about their local district's patterns. Once they decide Chapter 7 is the right path, they can use Upsolve to prepare their filing. The two platforms complement each other well.

Frequently Asked Questions

Is Upsolve really free?

Yes, Upsolve is a nonprofit that offers a free Chapter 7 bankruptcy filing tool. They are funded by grants and donations. However, their tool only covers Chapter 7 and requires you to enter personal financial information into their app.

Is Open Bankruptcy Project really free?

Yes, OBP is a 501(c)(3) nonprofit. All tools, data, and research are free and open source. OBP does not collect personal information and does not prepare bankruptcy filings.

Can I use Upsolve for Chapter 13?

No. Upsolve only supports Chapter 7 bankruptcy filings. If you need Chapter 13, Chapter 11, or Chapter 12 information, OBP covers all chapters through its research network.

Which is better for bankruptcy research?

OBP is primarily a research platform with 4.9 million federal cases analyzed across 94 districts. Upsolve is primarily a filing tool. If you need data, statistics, or educational content, OBP is the better fit. If you need help preparing Chapter 7 forms, Upsolve is the better fit.

Does Upsolve collect my personal information?

Yes, Upsolve's filing tool requires you to enter detailed personal and financial information to generate your bankruptcy forms. OBP's screening and research tools work with aggregated federal data and never ask for personal information.

The Bottom Line

Upsolve is best for filing Chapter 7. OBP is best for research across all chapters. Use both.
