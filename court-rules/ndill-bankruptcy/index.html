U.S. Bankruptcy Court for the Northern District of Illinois - Local Rules Guide 2026

First published 2026-05-22/Last modified 2026-05-22/CC-BY-4.0

Abstract

The United States Bankruptcy Court for the Northern District of Illinois (Bankr. N.D. Ill.) sits as a unit of the United States District Court for the Northern District of Illinois under 28 U.S.C. § 151. The court is among the largest-volume bankruptcy filing venues in the United States. Its local rules are codified as the Local Bankruptcy Rules for the Northern District of Illinois (L.R. 1001-1 through 9075-1) and are supplemented by an extensive collection of standing orders and judge-specific procedures. This guide summarizes the local-rule features that most often diverge from federal default practice in the district.

Court Identification and Coverage

The Northern District of Illinois comprises 18 counties in the northern third of Illinois, including the Chicago metropolitan area. The bankruptcy court maintains divisional offices at:

  • Chicago (Eastern Division) - Everett McKinley Dirksen United States Courthouse, 219 South Dearborn Street, Chicago, IL 60604. Principal courthouse and clerk's office for the bulk of the district's bankruptcy filings.
  • Rockford (Western Division) - Stanley J. Roszkowski United States Courthouse, 327 South Church Street, Rockford, IL 61101. Serves the western counties of the district.

Bankruptcy judges are Article I judges appointed under 28 U.S.C. § 152 to fourteen-year terms by the United States Court of Appeals for the Seventh Circuit. The court's chief-judge designation and full judicial roster are published on the court's website.

Local Rule Numbering Scheme

The court uses the standard four-digit scheme keyed to the Federal Rules of Bankruptcy Procedure:

  • L.R. 1007-1 - schedules, statements, and lists
  • L.R. 2014-1 - employment of professionals
  • L.R. 2016-1 - compensation of professionals
  • L.R. 2091-1 - withdrawal of appearance
  • L.R. 3015-1 - Chapter 13 plans
  • L.R. 9013-1 - motion practice generally
  • L.R. 9037-1 - redaction and restriction of public access

Attorney Admission

Practice before the Bankr. N.D. Ill. requires admission to the bar of the United States District Court for the Northern District of Illinois (general bar) or trial bar admission for certain proceedings. The district court's general bar admission rules are at N.D. Ill. L.R. 83.10 and following. The bankruptcy court accepts as standing admission an attorney's good-standing admission to the general bar of the district court. Pro hac vice admission is available on motion of local counsel of record, who remains responsible for compliance with local rules throughout the engagement.

Each attorney filing electronically must obtain an individual CM/ECF login from the clerk's office. Shared logins are prohibited.

Motion Practice and Hearings

L.R. 9013-1 prescribes the form and notice requirements for motions, including the standard notice and hearing procedures used across the district. Most motions are noticed for hearing on the assigned judge's calendar with a notice period derived from FRBP 2002 and any judge-specific procedure. Routine matters such as motions for relief from stay, motions to extend the deadline for objections to discharge, and unopposed extensions of bar dates are commonly resolved on negative notice.

Each judge of the Bankr. N.D. Ill. publishes chambers procedures setting out the judge's preferences for proposed orders, hearing setup, and emergency motion practice. The judge-specific procedures are posted on the court's website and frequently update; counsel should review them at the outset of each new case.

Hearings may be held in person, by telephone, or by video, depending on the assigned judge and the nature of the matter. Evidentiary hearings and contested confirmation hearings are typically conducted in person at the appropriate divisional courthouse.

Withdrawal of Appearance: L.R. 2091-1

L.R. 2091-1 governs withdrawal of an attorney's appearance. The rule requires:

  • A motion served on the client and on all parties who have entered an appearance in the case.
  • Court approval before the withdrawal becomes effective.
  • In cases where the client is a corporation, LLC, partnership, or other artificial entity, substitution of successor counsel before withdrawal is granted, consistent with the federal common-law rule that artificial entities cannot proceed pro se.

The rule applies to the individual attorney's appearance. A firm's employment under 11 U.S.C. § 327, where applicable, is governed by the order of employment and is separately terminable.

Employment of Professionals and Fee Disclosure

L.R. 2014-1 supplements FRBP 2014 by requiring a verified statement disclosing all connections to parties in interest, the debtor, the United States Trustee, and any person employed in the office of the United States Trustee. The court enforces strictly the supplemental-disclosure obligation when circumstances change.

Compensation applications under L.R. 2016-1 must comply with the U.S. Trustee's compensation guidelines and itemize time entries with sufficient particularity to permit independent review. The court routinely sets fee applications for hearing in Chapter 11 and Subchapter V cases regardless of whether objections have been filed.

FRBP 2016(b) requires debtor's counsel to file a statement disclosing compensation paid or agreed to be paid in connection with the case and to file a supplemental statement within 14 days of any subsequent payment or agreement not previously disclosed. The district enforces the supplemental-disclosure obligation strictly across all chapters.

Chapter 13 Practice: L.R. 3015-1 and the Model Plan

The Bankr. N.D. Ill. handles one of the largest Chapter 13 dockets in the country. L.R. 3015-1 governs Chapter 13 plan practice and is supplemented by the court's model Chapter 13 plan form, which counsel filing in the district are expected to use absent good cause. Specific Chapter 13 trustees serve the Eastern and Western Divisions, and each trustee's office maintains its own administrative procedures consistent with the local rules.

The model plan format simplifies confirmation of routine plans by standardizing the placement of plan-specific elections, treatment of secured claims, and treatment of priority claims. Deviation from the model plan must be flagged in the body of the plan and explained on the record.

Electronic Filing (CM/ECF)

All documents must be filed electronically through CM/ECF except those specifically excluded by standing order or judge-specific procedure. The court's CM/ECF system is at ecf.ilnb.uscourts.gov. Document size is capped at the court's published ECF limit per upload. Filings must comply with FRBP 9037 redaction requirements; L.R. 9037-1 supplements with district-specific procedures for restricting public access and sealing.

Standing Orders and General Orders

The court maintains an extensive collection of standing and administrative orders. Active categories include:

  • Administrative Orders governing electronic filing and CM/ECF use
  • Standing orders implementing the Chapter 13 model plan and confirmation procedures
  • Standing orders addressing case-management procedures during exigent circumstances
  • Standing orders implementing Federal Rule changes at the district level
  • Judge-specific procedures published on the court's website that supplement generic local-rule procedures

The complete list of standing and administrative orders is posted on the court's website. Counsel should consult the current catalogue before relying on any federal default procedure.

Practical Impact for Counsel

Three features of N.D. Ill. bankruptcy practice most often surprise counsel from other districts:

Judge-specific procedures vary substantially. Each judge publishes chambers procedures with material differences in motion practice, proposed-order format, and emergency-motion handling. Counsel should treat the judge's published procedures as binding alongside the local rules.

The Chapter 13 model plan is the default expectation. Counsel filing in the Eastern Division should anticipate using the model plan form unless there is a documented reason to deviate. The Chapter 13 trustees enforce the model-plan expectation through their confirmation review process.

Two-division court with shared CM/ECF. The Chicago and Rockford divisions operate on a unified CM/ECF system but with different judge assignments and somewhat different scheduling practices. Counsel should confirm the assigned judge's division-specific schedule when planning hearings.
