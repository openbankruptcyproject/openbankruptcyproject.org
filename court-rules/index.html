Bankruptcy Court Local Rules

The federal Bankruptcy Code and the Federal Rules of Bankruptcy Procedure apply nationwide, but every U.S. bankruptcy court also maintains a set of local rules, general orders, and standing orders that govern day-to-day practice. Local rules cover everything from chambers procedures and required form documents to motion calendars, ECF filing conventions, and judge-specific preferences.

About the bankruptcy court system

Bankruptcy cases are heard in the United States Bankruptcy Courts, which are units of the U.S. District Courts established under 28 U.S.C. § 151. There are 94 federal judicial districts and a corresponding bankruptcy court for each. Bankruptcy judges serve fourteen-year terms under 28 U.S.C. § 152 and may be designated to hear cases anywhere within their circuit.

Although the Bankruptcy Code and Federal Rules of Bankruptcy Procedure supply the substantive framework, each bankruptcy court issues its own local rules under Bankruptcy Rule 9029. Local rules typically cover ECF filing conventions, chambers copies, hearing calendars, mediation programs, fee application formatting, presumptive procedures for cash collateral and DIP financing, and Chapter 13 plan-form standards. Many districts also adopt judge-specific standing orders that vary within the same courthouse.

The guides below summarize the local procedural landscape for six high-volume districts. They are intended as orientation for practitioners and pro se filers unfamiliar with a district, not as substitutes for the official local rules text published on each court's own website.

District guides

  • Central District of California (C.D. Cal.)Five-division consumer-heavy district with extensive local form requirements and a robust mediation program.
  • District of Kansas (D. Kan.)Statewide single-district court with Topeka, Wichita, and Kansas City divisions and uniform Chapter 13 plan form.
  • Northern District of Illinois (N.D. Ill.)Chicago-based commercial-heavy district with detailed cash-collateral and complex-case procedures.
  • Southern District of New York (S.D.N.Y.)Manhattan-based mega-case venue with extensive complex-case orders and committee practice rules.
  • Southern District of Texas (S.D. Tex.)Houston-based district known for the complex-case panel and accelerated confirmation procedures.
  • Western District of Missouri (W.D. Mo.)Kansas City and Springfield divisions, with a model uniform Chapter 13 plan and active Subchapter V practice.
Verify before filing: Local rules change frequently. Confirm current text on each court's official website before relying on procedural details summarized in these guides.
