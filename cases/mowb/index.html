United States Bankruptcy Court for the Western District of Missouri

The United States Bankruptcy Court for the Western District of Missouri (W.D. Mo.) administers federal bankruptcy proceedings across the western half of Missouri, with divisions in Kansas City, Springfield, Joplin, St. Joseph, and Jefferson City. The court sits within the Eighth Circuit Court of Appeals and applies the federal Bankruptcy Code together with W.D. Mo. Bankruptcy Local Rules.

Section 01Court Overview

W.D. Mo. is one of two federal bankruptcy districts in Missouri, paired with the Eastern District of Missouri (E.D. Mo.) which covers the St. Louis region. W.D. Mo. covers 66 counties in the western half of the state, with the principal courthouse in Kansas City.

The court hears all chapters of bankruptcy authorized under the federal Bankruptcy Code: Chapter 7 (liquidation), Chapter 11 (business reorganization, including Subchapter V for small businesses), Chapter 12 (family farmer or fisherman), and Chapter 13 (wage-earner repayment plan). It also adjudicates adversary proceedings, contested matters, and motions ancillary to the main cases pending before it.

Section 03Local Rules & Filing

W.D. Mo. administers its bankruptcy local rules at the following location. Statutory federal filing fees are uniform across all US bankruptcy districts: $338 for Chapter 7, $313 for Chapter 13, $1,738 for Chapter 11, and $278 for Chapter 12 (current as of 2026).

The W.D. Mo. Bankruptcy Local Rules govern matters not addressed by the Federal Rules of Bankruptcy Procedure (FRBP) or the Bankruptcy Code, including filing requirements, attorney appearance and withdrawal procedures, and division-specific scheduling.

Local Rule 2091-1(A) - Attorney Withdrawal

W.D. Mo. Bankruptcy Local Rule 2091-1(A) governs attorney withdrawal from bankruptcy proceedings. The rule requires that an attorney of record may withdraw only by Court permission, by an order entered after service of notice of withdrawal on the client, other counsel of record, the trustee (if any), and the United States Trustee. An order will not be entered until new counsel enters an appearance, except for good cause.

This rule is significant because it defines the procedural requirements for both individual-attorney and firm-wide withdrawals. A motion to withdraw filed without complying with Rule 2091-1(A) (for example, without proper notice to the client or without addressing the appearance-of-new-counsel requirement) may not result in the relief requested. The rule is the procedural backbone for the obligation of attorneys to maintain continuity of representation through plan confirmation and post-confirmation administration unless properly relieved.

Section 04United States Trustee & Trustees

The Office of the United States Trustee for Region 13 administers federal trustee oversight of W.D. Mo. bankruptcy cases. The U.S. Trustee monitors case administration, reviews fee applications and disclosures of compensation under FRBP 2016, can appoint trustees and examiners, and brings motions to dismiss or convert cases when appropriate. The Subchapter V trustee panel for small-business reorganizations is administered by the U.S. Trustee's office under 11 U.S.C. § 1183.

Section 06Privacy Rule (FRBP 9037) Context

Federal Rule of Bankruptcy Procedure 9037 requires that Social Security Numbers, dates of birth, financial account numbers, and minor-child identifiers be redacted in bankruptcy filings. This rule applies uniformly across all US federal bankruptcy districts including W.D. Mo.

The Federal Judicial Center's April 2024 empirical study, Unredacted Social Security Numbers in Federal Court PACER Documents, found that 72% of identified SSNs in a sample of 2022 PACER filings appeared noncompliant with the privacy rules; the noncompliance rate in proof-of-claim filings specifically was 98%. The study did not break out compliance rates by district.

Primary source: Unredacted Social Security Numbers in Federal Court PACER Documents (FJC, April 2024).

Section 08Frequently Asked Questions

What is the United States Bankruptcy Court for the Western District of Missouri?

The United States Bankruptcy Court for the Western District of Missouri (W.D. Mo.) is the federal bankruptcy court covering the western half of Missouri, including the Kansas City, Springfield, Joplin, St. Joseph, and Jefferson City divisions. It sits within the Eighth Circuit Court of Appeals. The principal courthouse is at 400 East 9th Street, Kansas City, MO 64106.

What chapters of bankruptcy can be filed in W.D. Mo.?

Like all federal bankruptcy courts, W.D. Mo. handles Chapter 7 (liquidation), Chapter 11 (business reorganization, including Subchapter V for small businesses), Chapter 12 (family farmer or fisherman), and Chapter 13 (wage-earner repayment plan). The local rules for filing are administered at https://www.mow.uscourts.gov.

What is the United States Trustee Office for W.D. Mo.?

The Office of the United States Trustee for Region 13 administers oversight of W.D. Mo. bankruptcy cases. The U.S. Trustee monitors case administration, reviews fee applications, can appoint trustees and examiners, and brings motions to dismiss or convert cases when appropriate. Contact information is published at https://www.justice.gov/ust.

What is the Subchapter V trustee panel?

The Subchapter V trustee panel is a group of trustees appointed by the United States Trustee under 11 U.S.C. § 1183 to serve in small-business Chapter 11 cases that elect Subchapter V treatment. Sub V trustees are active fiduciaries with statutory duties to facilitate plan confirmation, monitor case administration, and report to the court on debtor and counsel conduct during the post-confirmation period. The W.D. Mo. Sub V trustee panel is administered by the U.S. Trustee's Region 13 office.
