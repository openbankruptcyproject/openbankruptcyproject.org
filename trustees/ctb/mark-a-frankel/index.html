Section 02Statutory Role

Bankruptcy trustees serve in several distinct statutory capacities: United States Trustees (28 U.S.C. § 581), Chapter 7 panel trustees (28 U.S.C. § 586(a)(1)), standing Chapter 13 trustees (28 U.S.C. § 586(b)), and Subchapter V trustees (28 U.S.C. § 586). Each role carries different statutory duties under Title 11. This page does not assert which role, if any, this individual currently holds; current appointments are maintained by the United States Trustee Program (see Supervising Office below).

Section 06Privacy Rule (FRBP 9037) Context

Federal Rule of Bankruptcy Procedure 9037 requires that Social Security Numbers, dates of birth, financial account numbers, and minor-child identifiers be redacted in bankruptcy filings. Trustees commonly encounter unredacted personal identifiers on proofs of claim, schedules, and exhibits. The Federal Judicial Center’s April 2024 empirical study, Unredacted Social Security Numbers in Federal Court PACER Documents, found that unredacted SSNs were rare overall (0.07% of bankruptcy documents) but were almost never exempt when they did appear on a proof of claim: of the unredacted SSNs the study identified in proof-of-claim filings, 98% were noncompliant with the privacy rules, compared with 72% across all bankruptcy filings. OBP cites the FJC study only as national context; OBP does not publish per-trustee or per-district Rule 9037 compliance numbers.

Primary source: Unredacted Social Security Numbers in Federal Court PACER Documents (FJC, April 2024).
