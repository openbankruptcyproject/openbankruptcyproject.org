Section 01What Chapter 13 Is

Chapter 13 of the United States Bankruptcy Code allows an individual with regular income to propose a 3 to 5 year repayment plan to creditors. A standing Chapter 13 trustee administers payments under 11 U.S.C. § 1302. On completion, debtors typically receive a discharge under 11 U.S.C. § 1328(a).

Who files Chapter 13: individual debtors with regular income whose unsecured debts fall below the limits set in 11 U.S.C. § 109(e). Common motivations include curing mortgage arrears, stripping wholly unsecured junior liens, and managing tax obligations through a court-supervised plan.

Typical timeline: A Chapter 13 plan must propose payments over 36 to 60 months depending on debtor income relative to applicable state median. Discharge under 11 U.S.C. § 1328(a) is entered after the trustee certifies completion of all plan payments. Dismissals and conversions to Chapter 7 are common when plans become infeasible.
