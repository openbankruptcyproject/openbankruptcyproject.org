Official Bankruptcy Form B121

Form B121: Statement About Your Social Security Numbers

The non-public form that carries the debtor's full SSN or ITIN, used by the court for identity verification and creditor notice while keeping the number off the public docket.

Purpose and statutory authority

Official Form B121 is the means by which an individual debtor provides the court with the debtor's full Social Security Number (or Individual Taxpayer Identification Number, or statement that no SSN/ITIN has been issued). The form supports identity verification by the trustee, accurate creditor notice, and credit-bureau reporting of the bankruptcy filing. It is treated as a non-public record under Fed. R. Bankr. P. 1007(f) and is not posted to the public docket on PACER.

The form coordinates with Fed. R. Bankr. P. 9037(a), which limits public filings to the last four digits of any SSN or ITIN. Only the last four digits appear on Form B101 and other publicly-filed documents; the full number is captured here.

Who must file

Every individual debtor. Joint debtors file a single B121 listing both spouses' full numbers. A debtor with no SSN and no ITIN checks the corresponding box and provides any other government-issued identification information available.

What the form requires

  • Each debtor's legal name as it appears on the petition
  • Each debtor's full Social Security Number or full ITIN
  • If no SSN or ITIN, an attestation to that fact
  • Signature(s) of debtor(s) under penalty of perjury

Filing context and handling

Form B121 is filed with the petition under Fed. R. Bankr. P. 1007(b)(7) and 1007(c). It is delivered to the clerk in physical or sealed-electronic form. The court uses it (a) to verify the debtor's identity at the meeting of creditors under Section 341(a) and Fed. R. Bankr. P. 4002(b)(1)(A) (where the trustee compares the number to the debtor's government-issued photo identification), and (b) to populate the notices that the Bankruptcy Noticing Center sends to creditors and to the major consumer reporting agencies.

The form is not docketed publicly. Trustees and the court access it under court-supervised conditions; it is not available through ordinary PACER queries.

Coordination with Rule 9037

Form B121 is the safe place for full SSNs in a bankruptcy case. All other public filings, including Form B101 (Voluntary Petition), Form B309 (Notice of Bankruptcy Case), Form B410 (Proof of Claim), and any attachment such as a contract or loan agreement, must be redacted to the last four digits of SSN/ITIN under Fed. R. Bankr. P. 9037(a). The two forms are designed to operate together: full number on B121, last four digits everywhere public.

Common pitfalls

  • Wrong number on B121. A typo prevents creditor matching and may require an amended B121 plus a notice to creditors who received the wrong number. Fed. R. Bankr. P. 1009(a) permits amendment as of right.
  • SSN appearing in full on other filings. Even though Form B121 is non-public, a filer who attaches a loan contract showing the full SSN to a proof of claim or motion has created a Rule 9037 exposure independent of B121.
  • Joint petition with only one SSN listed. Both spouses' full numbers must appear when a joint petition is filed.
  • Mismatch with government ID at Section 341 meeting. The trustee verifies the number against the debtor's photo ID; a discrepancy can adjourn the meeting and trigger a Rule 4002 examination.

See uscourts.gov/forms-rules/forms/bankruptcy-forms under the individual-debtor category. The form is short and changes infrequently.
