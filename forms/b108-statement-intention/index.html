Official Bankruptcy Form B108

Form B108: Statement of Intention for Individuals Filing Under Chapter 7

The debtor's election to reaffirm, redeem, surrender, or assume on each item of secured collateral and each leased personal property.

Purpose and statutory authority

Official Form B108 implements 11 U.S.C. Section 521(a)(2), which requires an individual Chapter 7 debtor whose schedules include debts secured by property of the estate to file a statement of intention specifying how the debtor will treat each secured debt. The form also captures the debtor's intentions on personal-property leases under Section 365(p).

The intention statement is not itself a binding agreement. It is a declaration of plan: the debtor commits to perform the elected option within the post-petition windows set by Section 521(a)(2)(B) and Section 362(h).

Who must file

Every individual Chapter 7 debtor with scheduled secured debt or scheduled personal-property leases. Chapter 11, 12, and 13 debtors do not file Form B108; their treatment of secured debt and leases is handled through the plan and through Section 365 motions.

The four options for each secured debt

Reaffirm

Sign a reaffirmation agreement under Section 524(c) excepting the personal liability on the debt from discharge. Court approval is required if the debtor is not represented by counsel; if represented, counsel's certification under Section 524(c)(3) is required.

Redeem

Pay the secured creditor the allowed secured claim amount in a single lump sum under Section 722. Available only for tangible personal property primarily for personal, family, or household use that is exempt or has been abandoned.

Surrender

Give the property back to the secured creditor. The discharge eliminates personal liability on any deficiency.

Other (rare)

The form contains an "Other" line. Strategies sometimes shown here (such as "ride-through" or "pay and retain") have been substantially curtailed by Section 521(a)(6) and Section 362(h) for cases in courts that do not recognize them.

Personal-property leases

For each scheduled lease of personal property, the debtor elects to assume under Section 365(p)(2) or to reject (vehicle leases are the most common example). Assumption requires the debtor to notify the lessor in writing; the lessor may set conditions for assumption consistent with the underlying lease.

Deadlines

  • File Form B108: within 30 days after the date of filing the petition, or by the date of the meeting of creditors under Section 341(a), whichever is earlier (Section 521(a)(2)(A)).
  • Perform the election: within 30 days after the first date set for the meeting of creditors (Section 521(a)(2)(B)).
  • Section 362(h) deadline: if the debtor does not timely file the statement, or does not timely perform, the automatic stay terminates as to the collateral and the property is no longer property of the estate (Section 362(h)(1)).

Consequences of inaction

Section 362(h) is self-executing. If the statement of intention is not filed within the statutory window, or the elected action is not performed within 30 days of the first meeting of creditors, the automatic stay terminates as to the collateral and the property leaves the estate. The trustee may file a motion under Section 362(h)(2) to find that the property is of consequential value or benefit to the estate to keep the stay in place.

For personal-property leases, failure to timely assume under Section 365(p) is treated as rejection, and the lessor may proceed under state law to recover the property.

Common pitfalls

  • Reaffirmation without the means. A reaffirmation that creates a presumption of undue hardship under Section 524(m) requires court approval; the debtor's Schedule J must show ability to pay.
  • "Retain and pay" with no court approval. Outside of the few circuits that historically allowed ride-through, a debtor who keeps collateral without reaffirming, redeeming, or curing through plan provisions risks Section 362(h) termination and post-discharge repossession.
  • Failure to update on amended schedules. If a creditor or collateral is added by amendment, the statement of intention must be amended within 30 days of the amendment under Fed. R. Bankr. P. 1019(1)(B) principles.
  • Lease assumption without lessor cooperation. The lessor may decline assumption or may condition it on cure of arrears; the debtor cannot force assumption unilaterally.
