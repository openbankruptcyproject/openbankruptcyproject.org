Official Bankruptcy Form B122C-1

Form B122C-1: Chapter 13 Statement of Current Monthly Income and Calculation of Commitment Period

The income side of Chapter 13 confirmation analysis: compute CMI, compare to the state median, and set the 3-year or 5-year applicable commitment period.

Purpose and statutory authority

Official Form B122C-1 implements the income computation for a Chapter 13 debtor under 11 U.S.C. Section 1325(b) and the definition of "current monthly income" at Section 101(10A). The form computes annualized CMI, compares to the state median family income for the household size, and determines the "applicable commitment period" of either three years (below-median) or five years (at- or above-median) under Section 1325(b)(4).

Where the debtor is at or above the state median, the debtor must also file Form B122C-2 (Calculation of Your Disposable Income), which applies the Section 707(b)(2)-style expense deductions to produce the projected disposable income figure the plan must commit if an unsecured creditor or the trustee objects under Section 1325(b)(1)(B).

Who must file

Every individual debtor in Chapter 13. Joint debtors file one combined B122C-1 covering household income.

How current monthly income is computed

  • Gross income from all sources averaged across the six full calendar months preceding the petition month
  • Includes wages, salary, business income, rental income, interest, dividends, pension and retirement income, and regular household contributions
  • Excludes benefits received under the Social Security Act, victims-of-war-crimes payments, victims-of-terrorism payments, and the other Section 101(10A)(B) exclusions
  • Non-filing spouse income is included as household income, with a marital-adjustment line for amounts not regularly contributed

Applicable commitment period

Below-median: 3 years

If annualized CMI is below the applicable state median family income for the debtor's household size, the applicable commitment period under Section 1325(b)(4)(A)(i) is three years. The plan may run longer for cause approved by the court but cannot exceed five years.

At- or above-median: 5 years

If annualized CMI equals or exceeds the applicable state median, the commitment period is five years under Section 1325(b)(4)(A)(ii). The five-year cap is absolute under Section 1322(d)(1).

Median-income comparison

The form references the United States Trustee Program's median family income tables at justice.gov/ust/means-testing. The applicable table is the one in effect on the petition date; USTP updates tables on the May 1 / November 1 cycle.

Filing context and deadlines

Form B122C-1 is filed with the petition or within 14 days under Fed. R. Bankr. P. 1007(b)(6) and (c). If above-median, Form B122C-2 is due on the same schedule. The confirmation hearing under Section 1324 is held not earlier than 20 days nor later than 45 days after the meeting of creditors, per Section 1324(b); the disposable-income analysis is the principal substantive confirmation issue raised by the trustee and unsecured creditors.

Common pitfalls

The applicable commitment period is a "temporal" floor, not just a "value" floor. Even where the debtor has zero projected disposable income, the Supreme Court's analysis in Hamilton v. Lanning and circuit-level treatment generally requires that the plan extend over the full applicable commitment period (3 or 5 years) unless the unsecured class is paid in full.

  • Household-size mismatch with Schedule J. A debtor who claims one household size on B122C-1 and a different size on Schedule J invites trustee scrutiny on both filings.
  • Non-recurring income included without explanation. Large one-time bonuses or settlement proceeds received in the six-month window can push a below-median debtor above the median; supplemental B122C-1 amendments or special-circumstances showings under Section 707(b)(2)(B) (incorporated by Section 1325) may be needed.
  • Stale median tables. A petition filed within days of a USTP table update should be checked against the date-of-filing table.
  • Self-employed business income. Business income is reported on B122C-1 net of ordinary and necessary operating expenses (not gross receipts); Schedule I-style gross-revenue figures may not match.
