Official Bankruptcy Form B122B

Form B122B: Chapter 11 Statement of Your Current Monthly Income

Income computation for an individual Chapter 11 debtor, feeding the projected-disposable-income confirmation requirement of Section 1129(a)(15).

Purpose and statutory authority

Official Form B122B captures current monthly income for an individual Chapter 11 debtor. The form supports the projected-disposable-income test for confirmation of an individual Chapter 11 plan under 11 U.S.C. Section 1129(a)(15), which (where an unsecured creditor objects) requires that the plan either pay the unsecured claim in full or commit projected disposable income (as defined by reference to Section 1325(b)(2)) over a five-year period.

The "B" in B122B reflects its position in the means-test family: B122A series for Chapter 7, B122B for Chapter 11, B122C series for Chapter 13. All three use the same Section 101(10A) definition of current monthly income but apply it to different confirmation and abuse-screening machinery.

Who must file

Every individual debtor in Chapter 11, including individual debtors who elect to be treated under Subchapter V of Chapter 11 (Section 1182). Non-individual Chapter 11 debtors (corporations, LLCs, partnerships) do not file Form B122B because Section 1129(a)(15) applies only to individuals.

Note: although the form's archival reference sometimes uses the phrase "non-individual" loosely (because the form is the income statement on the "B" track distinct from the "A" individual-Chapter-7 track and the "C" individual-Chapter-13 track), in practice Form B122B is the individual-Chapter-11 instrument. Non-individual Chapter 11 debtors are not subject to the personal-debtor means-test family.

How current monthly income is computed

The computation tracks Form B122A-1 closely:

  • Gross income from all sources averaged across the six full calendar months preceding the petition month
  • Includes wages, salary, business income, rental income, interest and dividends, pension and retirement income, regular household contributions from any source
  • Excludes Social Security Act benefits and the other categorical exclusions of Section 101(10A)(B)
  • Joint debtor income is combined; non-filing spousal income flows through the marital-adjustment line

Relationship to Section 1129(a)(15)

Form B122B is the income-side input to the Section 1129(a)(15) calculation, which is triggered only if the holder of an allowed unsecured claim objects to confirmation. Where triggered, the debtor must show either (A) that the plan pays the allowed unsecured claim in full as of the effective date, or (B) that the value of property to be distributed under the plan over the longer of five years or the plan term is not less than the debtor's projected disposable income.

"Projected disposable income" in this context borrows the Chapter 13 definition in Section 1325(b)(2): current monthly income minus amounts reasonably necessary for the debtor's and dependents' maintenance and support, charitable contributions up to 15% of gross income, and business-operations expenses where applicable.

Filing context and deadlines

Form B122B is filed with the petition or within 14 days under Fed. R. Bankr. P. 1007(b)(6) and (c). Confirmation is generally not earlier than the Section 1125 disclosure-statement process for traditional Chapter 11 cases, or the Section 1188 status-conference timeline for Subchapter V cases. The Form B122B figures inform the plan-feasibility and projected-disposable-income showings at confirmation.

Common pitfalls

  • Treating B122B as a presumption-of-abuse form. Unlike Chapter 7's Section 707(b)(2) machinery, Chapter 11 does not use the means-test result to dismiss the case for presumed abuse. The B122B output is a confirmation-stage input, not a screening filter.
  • Inconsistency with the operating-business projections. An individual Chapter 11 debtor running a small business (often the Subchapter V context) presents projected income via Form B122B and via the plan exhibits; consistency between the six-month historical CMI and the prospective projections is examined at confirmation.
  • Stale figures at confirmation. Where confirmation occurs months after filing, courts have sometimes required an updated Form B122B or supplemental projection if the underlying income has materially changed.
  • Misapplied Social Security exclusion. The exclusion is for benefits under the Social Security Act; private disability and pension income generally is not excluded.
