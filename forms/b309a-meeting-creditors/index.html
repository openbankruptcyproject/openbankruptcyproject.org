Official Bankruptcy Form B309A

Form B309A: Notice of Chapter 7 Bankruptcy Case (No Proof of Claim Required)

The court-issued notice that goes to every scheduled creditor in a Chapter 7 no-asset case, setting the meeting of creditors and announcing that no proofs of claim are required at this time.

Purpose and statutory authority

Official Form B309A is the standardized "Notice of Chapter 7 Bankruptcy Case" issued by the clerk of the bankruptcy court in a Chapter 7 case where the trustee's preliminary determination is that the case has no assets available for distribution to unsecured creditors. The notice implements 11 U.S.C. Section 342(a)'s requirement that creditors receive notice of the case and key deadlines, and Fed. R. Bankr. P. 2002(a)'s 21-day-minimum notice for the meeting of creditors and related deadlines.

The "A" variant in the 309 family is the no-asset Chapter 7 notice. The 309 family includes Form B309B (Chapter 7 asset case, proof-of-claim deadline included), B309C (Chapter 11 non-individual), B309D (Chapter 11 individual), B309E1/E2 (Chapter 11 Subchapter V), B309F1/F2 (Chapter 11 non-Subchapter V), B309G (Chapter 12), B309H (Chapter 13), and B309I (Chapter 13 with asset deadline).

Who issues and who receives

The clerk of the bankruptcy court (through the Bankruptcy Noticing Center) issues Form B309A. It is mailed to every creditor and party in interest listed on the debtor's creditor matrix, to the debtor, to the Chapter 7 trustee, and to the United States Trustee. Service is typically by U.S. mail; creditors may opt in to electronic noticing through the BNC.

Key information on the notice

Case identification

Case number, district, debtor name(s) and aliases, debtor address(es), last four digits of SSN/ITIN under Rule 9037(a), filing date, and chapter.

Trustee and meeting

Name and contact information for the appointed Chapter 7 trustee, date/time/location (or virtual access information) for the Section 341 meeting of creditors.

Discharge-objection deadline

Deadline for filing a complaint objecting to discharge under Section 727 or to determine the dischargeability of a debt under Section 523(c): 60 days after the first date set for the meeting of creditors, per Fed. R. Bankr. P. 4004(a) and 4007(c).

Exemption-objection deadline

Deadline for objections to claims of exemption under Fed. R. Bankr. P. 4003(b): 30 days after the conclusion of the meeting of creditors.

Automatic-stay notice

Statement that the filing of the bankruptcy case operates as an automatic stay under Section 362(a) prohibiting most collection efforts against the debtor and property of the estate.

No-asset claim language

Statement that the trustee believes there will be no property available for distribution from the estate, and that creditors therefore should not file a proof of claim at this time. If assets are later identified, a subsequent notice (Form B309B or equivalent) will set a bar date.

Filing context and timing

The notice issues shortly after the petition is filed and the trustee is appointed. The meeting of creditors must be held not less than 21 nor more than 40 days after the order for relief under Section 341(a) and Fed. R. Bankr. P. 2003(a). The notice therefore typically goes out within the first 7-14 days post-petition.

Common pitfalls

"No proof of claim required" is conditional. If the trustee later identifies assets, the court issues a separate "Notice of Possible Dividend" or a B309B-style notice setting a bar date. Creditors should monitor the docket for case-status changes; the no-asset notice is not a permanent disposition.

  • Missing creditor on the matrix. A creditor not listed on the debtor's creditor matrix does not receive the notice and may not be bound by the discharge for the listed-creditor purposes of Section 523(a)(3).
  • Stale address. Creditors with an address change should ensure the bankruptcy clerk has the current address on file, particularly for credit-card or medical-debt creditors whose servicers may relocate.
  • Confusing B309A with B309B. If you receive a B309B (asset case) or a follow-on Notice of Possible Dividend, the proof-of-claim deadline applies and missing it can foreclose distribution.
  • Discharge-objection deadlines run from the first-set meeting date. Continuances of the meeting of creditors do not extend the 60-day discharge-objection deadline unless an extension is timely sought under Fed. R. Bankr. P. 4004(b) or 4007(c).

Where to obtain the official current version

See uscourts.gov/forms-rules/forms/bankruptcy-forms under "Director's Procedural Forms" / "Notice forms." Note that the Form 309 family includes variants for every chapter and a Subchapter V split (E1/E2); using the wrong variant for the case chapter is a common clerk's-office issue corrected by re-noticing.
