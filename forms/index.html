Bankruptcy Forms Reference

The Official Bankruptcy Forms are the standardized petition, schedule, statement, and notice forms used in every federal bankruptcy case. They are promulgated by the Judicial Conference of the United States and published by the Administrative Office of the U.S. Courts. These reference pages cover ten of the most-filed forms with field-by-field guidance.

About the Official Bankruptcy Forms

Federal Rule of Bankruptcy Procedure 9009 requires that the Official Forms prescribed by the Judicial Conference be used without alteration except as permitted by the rules or by the forms themselves. The forms are renumbered with a "B" prefix and a three-digit identifier. The current Official Forms architecture was adopted as part of the 2015 forms modernization project and consolidated the older varied-letter scheme into a unified numerical system.

Individual debtors typically file Form B101 as the voluntary petition, the B106 series of schedules, the B107 Statement of Financial Affairs, the B108 Statement of Intention if Chapter 7, the B121 SSN statement, and one of the B122 means-test forms. The bankruptcy clerk subsequently issues the B309 series notice scheduling the section 341 meeting of creditors. Non-individual entities use the parallel non-individual forms (B201B, B106Sum-Nonindividual, B202, etc.).

All Official Forms are available without charge from the U.S. Courts website. The pages below describe each form's purpose, structure, common pitfalls, and the consequences of error or omission. They do not replace the form instructions published by the Administrative Office.

Form reference pages

  • Form B101 - Voluntary Petition for IndividualsThe petition that initiates a voluntary case for an individual debtor; demographic, chapter election, debt-type, and prior-case disclosures.
  • Form B106 - Individual Bankruptcy Schedules A/B through JThe asset, liability, executory contract, codebtor, income, and expense schedules together with the summary and declaration.
  • Form B107 - Statement of Financial Affairs for IndividualsIncome history, payments to creditors, lawsuits, transfers, closed accounts, business interests, and other prepetition financial activity.
  • Form B108 - Statement of Intention for Chapter 7The debtor's section 521(a)(2) election to retain, redeem, reaffirm, or surrender each item of property securing a consumer debt.
  • Form B121 - Statement About Your Social Security NumbersThe non-public statement filed with the petition that supplies the full SSN to the court for case-administration purposes under Rule 1007(f).
  • Form B122A-1 - Chapter 7 Statement of Current Monthly IncomeThe first step of the means test used to determine whether the section 707(b) presumption of abuse applies to an individual Chapter 7 case.
  • Form B122B - Chapter 11 Statement of Your Current Monthly IncomeThe current-monthly-income calculation required for individual Chapter 11 debtors to assist with applicable disposable-income analysis.
  • Form B122C-1 - Chapter 13 Statement of CMI and Commitment PeriodThe Chapter 13 current-monthly-income statement that determines the three- or five-year applicable commitment period.
  • Form B201A - Section 342(b) Notice to Individual DebtorsThe pre-filing notice required by section 342(b) describing the chapters and the consequences of selecting each.
  • Form B309A - Notice of Chapter 7 Case (No Proof of Claim Required)The clerk's notice to creditors of the filing, the 341 meeting date, and the dischargeability and discharge-objection deadlines.
Use current forms: The Official Forms are updated periodically. Always download the current version from the U.S. Courts forms site before filing.
