Official Bankruptcy Form B101

Form B101: Voluntary Petition for Individuals Filing for Bankruptcy

The opening document of an individual bankruptcy case, with required variants B101A (Initial Statement About Eviction Judgment) and B101B (Statement About Payment of Eviction Judgment).

Purpose and statutory authority

Official Form B101 is the Voluntary Petition that an individual files to commence a case under 11 U.S.C. Section 301. The filing of the petition is the act that creates the bankruptcy estate under 11 U.S.C. Section 541 and triggers the automatic stay under 11 U.S.C. Section 362. The form is promulgated under the rulemaking authority of 28 U.S.C. Section 2075 and the Federal Rules of Bankruptcy Procedure, and is prescribed by the Judicial Conference of the United States.

The petition is the legal instrument that asks the bankruptcy court to grant relief under a specified chapter of Title 11. By signing the petition, the debtor declares under penalty of perjury that the information is true and correct and acknowledges receipt of the required disclosures under 11 U.S.C. Section 342(b).

Who must file Form B101

Form B101 is used by individuals (including a married couple filing a joint petition) seeking relief under Chapter 7, Chapter 11, Chapter 12, or Chapter 13. A non-individual debtor (corporation, partnership, limited liability company) uses Form B201 instead. The form is filed in the United States Bankruptcy Court for the judicial district where the individual has had a domicile, residence, principal place of business, or principal assets for the longer portion of the 180 days preceding filing, per 28 U.S.C. Section 1408.

Eligibility to file under a particular chapter is governed by 11 U.S.C. Section 109. Among other requirements, an individual debtor must have received a credit-counseling briefing from an approved nonprofit budget and credit-counseling agency within the 180 days before filing, per Section 109(h). The petition includes a certification of that briefing.

Key sections of Form B101

Part 1 - Identification

Debtor's legal name, any other names used in the prior eight years, last four digits of the Social Security number (full SSN goes on the non-public Form B121), Employer Identification Number, addresses, and the district where the case is being filed.

Part 2 - Chapter election

The debtor checks the chapter under which relief is sought: 7, 11 (with Subchapter V election option for small business cases under Section 1182), 12, or 13.

Part 3 - Prior cases

List of all bankruptcy cases filed by the debtor or any spouse, business, or affiliate within the last eight years. This data feeds the analysis of discharge bars under Section 727(a)(8)/(9) and Section 1328(f).

Parts 4-9 - Property, debts, statistics

Summary of nature of debts (primarily consumer or business), estimated asset and liability ranges, estimated number of creditors, estimated funds available for unsecured creditors, and the credit-counseling certification.

Required variants B101A and B101B

Form B101A (Initial Statement About an Eviction Judgment Against You) is filed with the petition if a landlord has obtained a judgment for possession against the debtor. It implements the limited stay carve-out in 11 U.S.C. Section 362(b)(22) and (l). The debtor must also deposit any rent that would become due within 30 days post-petition with the clerk of court. Form B101B (Statement About Payment of an Eviction Judgment) is then filed within 30 days to certify cure.

Filing context and deadlines

Form B101 is the document whose filing commences the case. Several other documents must accompany the petition or be filed within statutory windows after filing under Fed. R. Bankr. P. 1007:

  • Form B121 (Statement About Your Social Security Numbers) - with the petition
  • Form B106 series (Schedules A/B through J) - within 14 days, extendable for cause
  • Form B107 (Statement of Financial Affairs) - within 14 days
  • Form B108 (Statement of Intention, Chapter 7 only) - within 30 days post-petition or by the meeting of creditors, whichever is earlier
  • Form B122A-1 / B122B / B122C-1 (Means Test forms) - with the petition or within 14 days
  • Credit-counseling certificate (separate from the petition certification) - with the petition
  • Payment-advice copies covering the 60 days pre-petition - within 14 days, per Section 521(a)(1)(B)(iv)

Filing fee: $338 for Chapter 7, $1,738 for Chapter 11 (or $1,167 for individual Subchapter V), $278 for Chapter 12, $313 for Chapter 13 (as of 2026). The debtor may apply to pay the Chapter 7 fee in installments under Fed. R. Bankr. P. 1006(b), or to waive the Chapter 7 fee under 28 U.S.C. Section 1930(f) if household income is below 150% of the federal poverty guideline.

Common pitfalls

Inaccurate venue under 28 U.S.C. Section 1408. An individual who recently moved must look back across the full 180-day pre-filing window and file in the district where the longer portion was spent, not the district of current residence.

  • Omitted prior cases in Part 3. The eight-year look-back includes joint filings, dismissed cases, cases of related business entities, and cases of a current or former spouse. Omission can produce a Section 727(a)(4) false-oath issue or trigger automatic-stay limitations under Section 362(c)(3) or (c)(4).
  • Missing or stale credit-counseling certificate. The briefing must be within the 180 days before filing. Filings without a valid certificate are subject to dismissal under Section 109(h) and Fed. R. Bankr. P. 1007(b)(3).
  • Wrong chapter election. Individuals with primarily consumer debt face the means-test gatekeeping in Section 707(b); the chapter selected on the petition must be consistent with the means-test result on Form B122A or B122C.
  • Joint petition without joint eligibility. Only spouses may file a joint petition under Section 302; same-sex married couples qualify, but unmarried partners do not.
  • Failure to file B101A when eviction judgment exists. Omission of B101A and the corresponding deposit forfeits the limited stay extension that Section 362(l) makes available, and the landlord may proceed with the writ of possession.

Where to obtain the official current version

Always use the current official form. Official Bankruptcy Forms are revised periodically. The current version is available free at the Administrative Office of the United States Courts forms library: uscourts.gov/forms-rules/forms/bankruptcy-forms. The direct link for Form B101 and its instructions is at uscourts.gov/forms/individual-debtors/voluntary-petition-individuals-filing-bankruptcy.
