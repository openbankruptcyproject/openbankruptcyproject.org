Official Bankruptcy Form B201A

Form B201A: Notice Required by 11 U.S.C. Section 342(b) for Individuals Filing for Bankruptcy

The mandatory written notice that must be given to a consumer debtor before the petition is filed, summarizing the chapters and the consequences of bankruptcy.

Purpose and statutory authority

Official Form B201A is the standardized written notice that 11 U.S.C. Section 342(b) requires a clerk of court (and, when an attorney is involved, the attorney) to give to an individual consumer debtor before a petition is filed. The notice describes the four chapters under which an individual may file, summarizes the services available from credit-counseling agencies, advises about debt-relief-agency disclosures, and warns about bankruptcy crimes under 18 U.S.C. Section 152 and related provisions.

The notice is one of two consumer-protection disclosures the 2005 Bankruptcy Abuse Prevention and Consumer Protection Act added at filing. The companion is Form B2030 (Disclosure of Compensation of Attorney for Debtor) under Section 329.

Who must give and receive the notice

The clerk of the bankruptcy court must give the notice to any individual whose debts are primarily consumer debts under Section 342(b). When the debtor is represented by an attorney, the attorney also delivers the notice as part of the pre-filing intake. The debtor's receipt of the notice is confirmed on the petition itself (Form B101, Part 5), where the debtor signs an acknowledgment.

What the notice contains

Chapter 7 - Liquidation

Explanation of the liquidation process, eligibility under the means test, the trustee's role, and the discharge.

Chapter 13 - Adjustment of debts

Plain-English description of the 3- to 5-year plan structure, the role of the standing trustee, and the eligibility limits under Section 109(e).

Chapter 11 - Reorganization

Short description of the reorganization process, with note that individuals may proceed under Chapter 11 (including Subchapter V where eligible).

Chapter 12 - Family farmer or fisherman

Short description of the specialized adjustment process available to family farmers and family fishermen with regular annual income meeting the Section 101(18)/(19A) thresholds.

Additional content

  • Statement that bankruptcy is a serious step with long-term consequences
  • Notice of credit-counseling and debtor-education requirements under Section 109(h) and Section 727(a)(11) / 1328(g)
  • Notice of bankruptcy-crime exposure for false statements, concealment of assets, and similar conduct
  • Notice of debt-relief-agency status and disclosure obligations under Sections 526-528 (if applicable to the assisting professional)
  • Notice of the right to file pro se

Filing context and deadlines

Section 342(b) requires the notice to be given before the petition is filed. The debtor's receipt acknowledgment appears on the petition. Fed. R. Bankr. P. 1007(b)(3) also requires the debtor to file a certificate from an approved credit-counseling agency under Section 521(b)(1); that is a separate document from the 342(b) notice but is closely associated in the consumer-debtor intake workflow.

Common pitfalls

Stale notice version. Form B201A has been revised since 2005 to reflect statutory amendments. Using an out-of-date version (for example, one that omits the Subchapter V reference or uses superseded dollar thresholds) is the principal compliance problem at filing.

  • Notice not given pre-filing. Where the debtor is represented, the attorney's intake checklist should document delivery of B201A before petition signature.
  • Acknowledgment box on B101 left unchecked. An incomplete acknowledgment on the petition produces a clerk's deficiency notice and may delay docketing.
  • Wrong notice used for non-consumer-debt debtor. An individual whose debts are primarily non-consumer is not strictly subject to Section 342(b), but most practitioners deliver the notice anyway to support a clean record.

Where to obtain the official current version

See uscourts.gov/forms-rules/forms/bankruptcy-forms under "Director's Procedural Forms." Note that the official numbering family for the 342(b) notice is the 200-series and the variant for individuals is B201A; B201B is reserved for non-individual notices where applicable.
