Federal

Suggestions 26-BK-3 and 26-BK-5 - Advisory Committee on Bankruptcy Rules

Judicial Conference of the United States - March 2026
OBP submitted an empirical analysis of statutory discharge bar enforcement gaps to the Advisory Committee on Bankruptcy Rules. The submission, based on analysis of 4.9 million federal bankruptcy cases, proposed automated screening of Sections 1328(f), 727(a)(8), and 109(g) discharge bars at the point of case filing. The suggestion was accepted on March 23, 2026 and assigned designation 26-BK-3. Cited by the Consumer Subcommittee in the April 15 Advisory Committee agenda book (Tab 4C, p.125, fn.4).

Federal

Suggestion 26-BK-5 - Advisory Committee on Bankruptcy Rules

Judicial Conference of the United States - April 2026
OBP submitted a supplemental empirical analysis addressing Federal Rule of Bankruptcy Procedure 9037(a) compliance gaps in Official Forms 121 and 309E1, based on a free RECAP/CourtListener scan identifying unredacted personal identifiers in filings across multiple federal judicial districts. The submission proposed five amendments: (1) an auto-sealing event code for documents flagged under Rule 9037; (2) template-integrity audit for Official Forms containing PII fields; (3) pre-acceptance screening at the ECF upload point; (4) a non-cure provision clarifying that redaction after PACER publication does not remedy prior disclosure; and (5) mandatory clerk notification to affected individuals. The suggestion was accepted on April 16, 2026 and assigned designation 26-BK-5. Forwarded to the Chairs and Reporters of both the Advisory Committee on Bankruptcy Rules and the Standing Committee.

Open-Source Tools & Data

Open Source

Bankruptcy Discharge Screener

GitHub - Live at 1328f.com
The first open-source tool for screening Section 1328(f) and 727(a)(8) discharge bars using client-side computation. Runs entirely in the browser - no data is transmitted to any server. The screener uses sql.js to query a local copy of the FJC database directly in the user's browser.

How to Cite OBP

If you reference our research, data, or tools in academic work, legal filings, journalism, or policy documents, we suggest the following citation format:

Open Bankruptcy Project, [Title of Specific Analysis or Tool], https://openbankruptcyproject.org/[page] (last visited [date]).

For the FJC dataset specifically:

Federal Judicial Center, Integrated Database - Civil (Bankruptcy), https://www.fjc.gov/research/idb (data accessed [date]), as analyzed by the Open Bankruptcy Project.
