Why this is free when PACER charges

PACER (Public Access to Court Electronic Records) is the official federal court records system. It charges $0.10 per page (capped at $3.00 per document) for everything except the docket-search page itself. PACER's fees have been controversial — the courts collect over $145 million per year from a system Congress originally intended to be self-sustaining only, not profitable.

The case captions, numbers, chapters, and filing dates we index are public information published by the courts. Florida Star v. B.J.F., 491 U.S. 524 (1989), and Cox Broadcasting Corp. v. Cohn, 420 U.S. 469 (1975), protect accurate publication of lawfully-obtained public-record information as constitutionally-protected speech. We publish that information at no cost. We do not republish PACER documents; we link to the free RECAP Archive (operated by the Free Law Project) where documents have been contributed.

Frequently asked questions

How do I find out if someone has filed bankruptcy?

Bankruptcy filings are public records. You can search them through PACER (the federal court system, which charges fees) or through a free public-records index like the Open Bankruptcy Project. Type the person's full name or business name in the search box above.

Is bankruptcy information public?

Yes. Federal bankruptcy filings are public records under 11 U.S.C. § 107. Anyone can view the docket, including case caption, chapter, filing date, and most documents. Personal identifiers (SSN, financial account numbers, dates of birth, minor children's names) must be partially redacted under FRBP 9037.

Is this free?

Yes. Our search is completely free. PACER (the official federal records system) charges $0.10 per page; we publish the public-record case captions and link to free RECAP/CourtListener archives where available.

Can I search by case number instead of name?

Yes. The search accepts both debtor names and case numbers. Try formats like "24-12345" or "4:2024bk40010".

What if the person I'm looking for doesn't appear?

Either they haven't filed bankruptcy, they filed in a court not yet in our index, or the filing is recent enough that we haven't synced it. Try PACER directly if you need definitive coverage.

What about state court (non-bankruptcy) filings?

We only index federal bankruptcy filings. For state court records, use the relevant state court's online docket system (most states have one).
